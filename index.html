Year one · Guiding principles

Five pillars of our work

Every program, partnership, and analysis is grounded in these principles.

01

Regions

Regions exist at many scales, can be nested within one another, and often overlap across ecological and political boundaries.

02

Resilience

The ability to bounce back from impact—fast and hard, or slow and firm—while adapting so future impacts are avoided.

03

Habitat connectivity

A vital ingredient of ecological integrity, sustaining wildlife movement, genetic exchange, and ecosystem function across working landscapes.

04

Human needs

Economic vitality and community resilience are defined by the people of a place, and vary dramatically across regions globally.

05

Collaborative web

Most actions toward regional resilience result from stakeholders and decisionmakers interacting in a political process. Elevating that process is central to what we do.

2026 focus

Our approach

Facilitating the access and analysis of spatial data required for prudent land-use policy and planning.

Spatial synthesis & communication

Synthesizing spatial analyses and communicating results to bolster knowledge and enable wise decisions by communities and policymakers.

Core expertise 01

Wildlife habitat connectivity mapping

Regional web-mapping atlases

Our story

History

1995

Origins in Santa Barbara

A grassroots effort to map and protect core wildlife habitat areas and linkages—within the region and to neighboring regions.

1999

Conception Coast Project · 501(c)(3)

Incorporated and began delivering a Regional Conservation Guide plus extensive mapping, analysis, and data services to the community.

2007

Broadening scope

Expanded globally to test lessons learned. Entered standby while continuing to share insights and strategize for re-engagement.

2020

Became Regional Resilience Network

Adopted the RRN name to reflect multi-regional work and a broadened focus on ecological, economic, and community resilience.

2026

Reactivating

Coming off standby. Launching three core programs and reconnecting with communities ready to build resilience.
