Foundation

Year One Guiding Principles

Five interconnected principles that shape every project, partnership, and decision we make.

⬡

Regions

Regions exist at many scales, can be nested within one another, and often overlap across ecological and political boundaries.

↺

Resilience

The ability to bounce back from impact—fast and hard, or slow and firm—while adapting so future impacts are avoided.

⟷

Habitat Connectivity

A vital ingredient of ecological integrity, sustaining wildlife movement, genetic exchange, and ecosystem function across working landscapes.

◎

Human Needs

Economic vitality and community resilience are defined by the people of a place, and vary dramatically for regions globally.

⬡

Collaborative Web

Most actions toward regional resilience result from stakeholders and decisionmakers in a political process. Elevating that process is central to our work.

2026 Focus

Our Approach

Our primary approach for 2026 is to facilitate the access and analysis of spatial data required for prudent land-use policy and planning.

01

Spatial Synthesis & Communication

Synthesizing spatial analyses and communicating these syntheses to bolster knowledge and enable wise decisions.

02

Wildlife Habitat Connectivity Mapping

Mapping the needs for ecological integrity—with a specific core expertise in wildlife habitat connectivity.

03

Regional Web-Mapping Atlases

Providing communities with regional atlases that meet their needs for data ownership, control, and interaction.

What We Do

Initial Programs

Three programs form the core of our current work, each designed to fill critical gaps in how communities access and act on spatial knowledge.

Core Program

The Linkage Mapper Project

Fiscally Sponsored Partner Organization

A flagship initiative providing wildlife habitat connectivity mapping services—including Synoptic Connectivity Modeling—to support land-use planning across regions. Linkage Mapper Services forms a major component of this project, translating complex spatial data into actionable conservation intelligence.

Highlight: Synoptic Connectivity Modeling — broad-scale, landscape-level connectivity analysis that informs regional planning decisions.
Service

Consulting Program

Applying all of our land-use planning and policy expertise to client needs—from resilience mapping to urban planning. Similar in scope to Linkage Mapper Services, but tailored to the full range of our spatial analysis capabilities.

Platform

The Regional Atlas Program

Available as a standalone service or bundled as a Linkage Mapper Service. Built on Principles of Knowledge Sharing Integrity, our atlases are designed to share mapped data across platforms—with several platforms currently under evaluation, including Data Basin with custom enhancements developed specifically for our work.

Example: Santa Barbara County Conservation Blueprint, 2019 — co-created by Dr. Gallo

Our Story

History

1995

Origins in Santa Barbara

Regional Resilience Network began in Santa Barbara County, inspired by a grassroots effort to map and protect core wildlife habitat areas and the linkages connecting them—within the region and to neighboring regions.

1999

Conception Coast Project · 501(c)(3)

Founded as Conception Coast Project and incorporated as a 501(c)(3). Provided the community with a Regional Conservation Guide plus extensive mapping, analysis, and data services.

2007

Broadening Horizons

Branched out to explore the world and put lessons learned into practice. The organization entered standby, continuing to share insights and strategize for re-engagement.

2020

Becoming Regional Resilience Network

Adopted the RRN name to reflect an expanded mandate—work across multiple regions and a broader focus on resilience in all its dimensions.

2026

Coming Off Standby

Reactivating with renewed purpose. Launching the three core programs, building our team, and reconnecting with communities across regions.
